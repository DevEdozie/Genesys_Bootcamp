The Da Vinci Code Dan Brown Thriller The Da Vinci Code follows symbologist Robert Langdon and cryptologist Sophie Neveu after a murder in the Louvre Museum in Paris causes them to become involved in a battle between the Priory of Sion and Opus Dei over the possibility of Jesus Christ and Mary Magdalene having a child together.
Patriot Games Tom Clancy Thriller The novel focuses on Jack Ryan being the target of Irish terrorist group Ulster Liberation Army for thwarting their kidnapping attempt on the Prince and Princess of Wales in London.
Lord of the flies William Golding Allegorical novel The plot concerns a group of British boys who are stranded on an uninhabited island and their disastrous attempts to govern themselves. Themes include the tension between groupthink and individuality, between rational and emotional reactions, and between morality and immorality.
The Odyssey Homer Epic Poetry It follows the Greek hero Odysseus, king of Itacha, and his journey home after the Trojan War. After the war, which lasted ten years, his journey lasted for ten additional years, during which time he encountered many perils and all of his crewmates were killed. In his absence, Odysseus was assumed dead, and his wife Penelope and son Telemachus had to contend with a group of unruly suitors who are competing for Penelope's hand in marriage.
Animal Farm George Orwell Political sattire Animal Farm is a beast fable in the form of a satirical allegorical novella by George Orwell, first published in England on 17 August 1945. It tells the story of a group of farm animals who rebel against their human farmer, hoping to create a society where the animals can be equal, free and happy. Ultimately, the rebellion is betrayed, and under the dictatorship of a pig named Napoleon, the farm ends up in a state as bad as it was before.
Great Expectations Charles Dickens Bildungroman The novel is set in Kent and London in the early to mid-19th century and contains some of Charles Dickens's most celebrated scenes, starting in a graveyard, where the young Pip is accosted by the escaped convict Abel Magwitch. Great Expectations is full of extreme imagery -- poverty, prison ships and chains, and fights to the death -- and has a colourful cast of characters who have entered popular culture. These include the Miss Havishame, the beautiful but cold Estella, and Joe Gargery, the unsophisticated and kind blacksmith.
Nineteen Eighty-Four George Orwell Dystopian The story takes place in an imagined future in the year 1984, when much of the world is in perpetual war. Great Britain, now known as Airstrip One, has become a province of the totalitarian superstate Oceania, which is led by Big Brother, a dictorial leader supported by an intense cult of personality manufactured by the Party's Thought Police. Through the Ministry of Truth, the Party engages in omnnipresent government surveillance, historical negationism and constant propaganda to persecute individuality and independent thinking.
The Girl with all the gifts M.R Carey Dystopia The Girl with All the Gifts is a science fiction novel by M.R Carey, published in June 2014 by Orbit Books. It is based on his 2013 Edgar Award-nominated short story Iphigenia in Autis and was written concurrently with the screenplay for the 2016 film. It deals with a dystopian future in which most of humanity is wiped out by a zombie-like fungal infection.
Ender's Game Orson Scott Card Science Fiction Ender's Game is a 1985 miltary science fiction novel by American author Orson Scott Card. Set at an unspecified date in Earth's future, the novel presents an imperilled humankind after two conflicts with an insected alien species they dub the buggers. In preparation for an anticipated third invasion, Earth's international military force recruits young children, including the novel's protagonist Andrew Ender Wiggin to be trained as elite officers.The children learn military strategy and leadership by playing increasingly difficult war games, including some in zero gravity, where Ender's tactical genius is revealed.

In the United States, it is widely read in high schools and middle schools. The novel deals with the serious issues of rape and racial ineqaulity, it is renowed for it's warmth and humor.
