Ojiakor Michael

I am a student 400 level student of Electronics and Computer Engineering at Nnamdi Azikiwe University Awka, Anambra State. I became interested in programming during my first year in the University. I was originally offered admission to study Electrical Engineering but during my first year, federal universities embarked on a nationwide strike and I spent that period at my grandma's place in my hometown. During the course of my stay there, I took interest in a particular show which was being aired on Nickolodeon, it was titled Game Shakers. It was about a group of kids who created video games and sold them to the public, I was really fascinated about the coding part of it. I was amazed that someone could actually sit down to create awesome stuff from the comfort of their desk and also, I felt the art of coding itself was cool.

I returned to school and continued with my education, I didn't begin active coding until after my first year in university, the exams were over and I needed something to keep busy with. I picked up programming books and started reading them. Luckily enough, we were to offer an Introduction to Programming (CSC 201) course the next semester, I was lucky to come across the book and I started studying it. It taught basic C++ and I studied it while writing the code on paper. My first real experience at writing real code was in that same semester, our seniors organized a bootcamp to teach us some tech skills. I enrolled for the program alongside my friend, I chose the Android development track. We were taught the basics of the Java programming language and also of Android development. It was my first introduction to programming and I found it really difficult though I completed the program succesfully.

Luckily for me, the Covid-19 pandemic broke out and we were all forced to leave the school premises, I returned to my home town and this time I downloaded a book called Java for Dummies which I used to learn the fundamentals of programming alongside a Java IDE I downloaded on my mobile phone. I later got a laptop and continued my development. I built some apps which i did not publish on the playstore. I continued moving at my pace until I applied for Google Africa Devloper Scholarship Program 2022. I enrolled in the Android Development Track and I was taught Android development properly. The program ran for 6 months, I completed it and got the certificate of participation.

After completing the program, I tried looking for jobs but could not find any available roles. I finally found one as a Front end Developer intern at Mactavis Digital a digital solution company in Lagos. I had to accept the role as I needed a company to perform my Industrial training for my 400 level internship period. As they say, no knowledge is a waste, I am currently enrolled in this program to improve my front end development skills and gain the best out of it.
